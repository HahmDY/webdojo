Classic Chocolate Chip Cookie Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/4 cups (280g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup (230g) unsalted butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup (150g) granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup (165g) packed brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups (340g) semi-sweet chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a large bowl, beat the butter and both sugars together until light and fluffy (about 2-3 minutes).
  4. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing just until combined.
  6. Fold in the chocolate chips.
  7. Drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto the prepared baking sheets, leaving about 2 inches of space between each cookie.
  8. Bake for 9-11 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden but the centers still look slightly underbaked.
  9. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheets for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Notes:

  • For chewier cookies, chill the dough for at least 24 hours before baking.
  • These cookies freeze well. Place cooled cookies in an airtight container and freeze for up to 3 months.
  • You can also freeze the dough. Form into balls, place on a baking sheet, freeze until solid, then transfer to a zip-top bag.

Variations:

  • Add 1 cup of chopped nuts (pecans, walnuts, or almonds) for added texture.
  • Replace chocolate chips with white chocolate chips, butterscotch chips, or a combination.
  • Add 1/2 teaspoon of cinnamon to the dry ingredients for a subtle spiced flavor.
